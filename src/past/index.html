My Past Life

Cleanroom Process Engineer

Until 2016 I was a cleanroom process engineer working for ECS Partners; the commercial unit of the Southampton Nanofabrication Centre (SNC). The SNC is a facility run by the University of Southampton’s School of Electronics and Computer Science. In this role I applied four years of fabrication experience gained during my PhD to commercial projects for companies requiring cleanroom fabrication.

I am experienced in numerous cleanroom fabrication and processing techniques using samples and full wafers with diameters ranging from 50-200mm of materials including silicon, silicon-carbide, germanium, gallium-arsenide, sapphire, glass and pyrex. I have carried out countless depositions of various metallic or dielectric films through e-beam evaporation, sputtering and PECVD and have performed thermal oxidations and anneals using quartz-tube furnaces. I am also proficient in dry etching RIE, ICP and IBE methods, wet chemical etching and cleaning..

I have extensive expertise in manual and automated, robotic, lithographic machines and lithography procedures, using a variety of positive and negative resists and have completed projects requiring up to ten masks. I am also experienced in numerous analysis tools such as optical microscope or SEM imaging, profiler or ellipsometer thickness measurements, or surface resistivity measurements and characterisation equipment including DC and RF probe stations, and hall-effect measurement tools.

As part of the first batch of users of the SNC I was one of the first users of a number of new tools and thus encountered and remedied many of the initial problems. As super-user for the two e-beam evaporators I developed evaporation parameters and recipes for aluminium, chromium, nickel and silver, and was responsible for up-keep and user training until a technician was hired. I also developed and characterised an aluminium wet etch as well as recipes for various optical lithography resists.

Southampton Nanofabrication Centre

The Southampton Nanofabrication Centre is home to the best set of nanoelectronics and photonics fabrication capabilities in the UK. It is also one of the premiere cleanrooms in Europe with its uniquely broad range of equipment, those of which I am experienced in are listed as follows.
